Safe Browsing Practices

If you are worried that someone might see what you are looking at online, follow these steps to protect your privacy.

1. Use the "Quick Exit" Button

The ⚡ EXIT NOW ⚡ button is at the top right of every page on this site.

  • What it does: Instantly takes you to Google.com.
  • When to use it: If someone walks into the room or you feel unsafe while browsing.
  • Note: This does NOT clear your browser history. You must do that manually.

2. Use Incognito / Private Mode

This is the safest way to browse. It prevents the browser from saving your history, cookies, or search data.

How to open it:

  • Chrome / Edge: Press Ctrl + Shift + N (Mac: Cmd + Shift + N)
  • Firefox: Press Ctrl + Shift + P (Mac: Cmd + Shift + P)
  • Safari (iPhone/Mac): Tap the tabs icon, then tap "Private".

3. How to Clear Your History

If you have already browsed this site without Private Mode, you should clear your history.

On Desktop (Chrome, Firefox, Edge):

  1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Delete.
  2. Select "Last hour" or "All time".
  3. Check "Browsing history" and "Cookies".
  4. Click "Clear data".

On iPhone (Safari):

  1. Open Settings.
  2. Scroll down to Safari.
  3. Tap Clear History and Website Data.

On Android (Chrome):

  1. Open Chrome and tap the three dots (⋮).
  2. Tap History > Clear browsing data.
  3. Select time range and tap Clear data.

4. Use Public Computers

If possible, use a computer at a public library. These are often set up to clear all data automatically when you log out. It is much safer than using a shared home computer or a personal phone that someone else might check.

Stay safe. If you are in immediate danger, call 112.
